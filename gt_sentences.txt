TOPIC 0
The Earth's climate has been changing at an unprecedented rate over the past century.
Greenhouse gas emissions, primarily carbon dioxide and methane, have significantly increased since the industrial revolution.
Human activities such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation are the primary drivers of global warming.
Scientists have recorded that global temperatures have risen by approximately 1.1°C since pre-industrial times.
The rise in temperature has caused glaciers and polar ice caps to melt at an alarming rate.
Rising sea levels threaten coastal communities worldwide and have already displaced millions of people.
Extreme weather events including hurricanes, droughts, and wildfires have become more frequent and severe.
The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) warns that warming must be limited to 1.5°C.
Agricultural systems are severely disrupted, causing widespread food insecurity in many vulnerable regions.
Biodiversity loss is accelerating due to habitat destruction and shifting climatic conditions.
The transportation sector is one of the largest contributors to greenhouse gas emissions across the globe.
Together, the shift from fossil fuels to renewable energy and electric transportation represents humanity’s best path forward against climate change.

TOPIC 1
Renewable energy refers to power derived from natural resources that replenish themselves continuously, for example sunlight and wind.
Solar power harnesses energy from the sun using photovoltaic cells or solar thermal collection systems.
Wind energy is generated by turbines that convert kinetic energy from the wind into electrical power.
Hydropower, which uses flowing water to spin turbines, is one of the oldest forms of renewable energy.
Geothermal energy taps heat stored within the Earth and provides a stable, consistent, and low-emission power supply.
The cost of solar and wind power has dropped by approximately 90 percent over the last decade alone.
Governments around the world are investing heavily in renewable energy infrastructure and large-scale grid modernization projects.
Energy storage technologies such as lithium-ion batteries are critical for managing the intermittent nature of renewable supply.
Smart grids enable more efficient and resilient distribution of electricity from diverse renewable energy sources.

TOPIC 2
Electrical vehicles (EVs) offer a transformative solution to significantly reduce carbon emissions from transportation.
Electric vehicles are powered by electric motors that draw energy from high-capacity rechargeable battery packs.
Modern electric vehicles can travel over 300 miles on a single charge, making them viable for everyday practical use.
Charging infrastructure is expanding rapidly with thousands of new public and private stations installed each year.
Battery technology is continually advancing with solid-state batteries expected to deliver greater range and improved safety.
Governments worldwide are offering incentives such as tax credits and direct subsidies to accelerate electric vehicle adoption.
Major automakers have announced plans to completely phase out internal combustion engines by approximately 2040.
The integration of electrical vehicles with smart renewable grids creates a powerful synergistic ecosystem for sustainable mobility.
Together, the shift from fossil fuels to renewable energy and electrical transportation represents humanity’s best path forward against climate change.
